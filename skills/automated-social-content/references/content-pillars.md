# Content Pillars Reference

Full breakdown of the 5-pillar system for the Marketing/Growth + Entrepreneurship niche, with evergreen topic ideas, Claude prompts per pillar, and hook formulas.

---

## The 5 Pillars

| Pillar | Default % | Emotional trigger | Best platforms |
|--------|-----------|------------------|----------------|
| Growth Tactics | 25% | Desire to improve, FOMO | LinkedIn, Twitter/X |
| Building in Public | 25% | Trust, relatability, connection | Twitter/X, LinkedIn, TikTok |
| Industry Insights | 20% | Authority, curiosity | LinkedIn, Twitter/X |
| Hot Takes | 20% | Controversy, validation | Twitter/X, TikTok, LinkedIn |
| Personal | 10% | Empathy, inspiration | TikTok, Instagram, LinkedIn |

Weights are configurable per workspace in the dashboard `/control` page. Zap 5 (weekly performance review) automatically recommends adjustments based on engagement data.

## Pillar 1 — Growth Tactics (25%)

**Goal:** Be the person who teaches tactical, actionable advice people can use immediately.

**What performs:** Specific frameworks, unexpected data points, "here's exactly how I did it" breakdowns, contrarian tactics.

### 10 Evergreen Topic Ideas

1. The exact landing page structure that improved our conversion rate by X%
2. 3 email sequences that generate leads on autopilot
3. How we reduced CAC by 40% without cutting ad spend
4. The retention framework no one talks about
5. Why most content calendars fail (and the 15-minute fix)
6. The A/B test that surprised us most this quarter
7. Our complete SEO workflow for a new blog post (step by step)
8. How to use social proof at every funnel stage
9. The 5-minute weekly review that keeps our growth on track
10. What the top 1% of B2B marketers do differently

### Claude Prompt for Growth Tactics

```
Write a [LinkedIn post / TikTok script / Twitter thread] about a specific growth tactic.

Rules:
- Open with a result: "We increased [metric] by [X]% in [timeframe]."
- Explain the specific tactic in 3-5 concrete steps
- Include one number or data point per step
- End with: "Here's the exact [framework/process/template] we use:"
- Pillar: Growth Tactics
- Tone: Expert friend, not guru
```

### Hook Formulas

- "I tested [tactic] for 90 days. Here's what actually worked:"
- "Most [professionals] ignore this [metric]. It's costing them [result]."
- "How [brand] grew from [X] to [Y] using one counterintuitive tactic:"

## Pillar 2 — Building in Public (25%)

**Goal:** Create trust by showing the real journey — wins, failures, process, and learnings.

**What performs:** Honest failure posts, milestone reveals, "what I'd do differently" retrospectives, raw numbers.

### 10 Evergreen Topic Ideas

1. Month X update: here's what the numbers look like
2. The mistake that cost us [X] and what I learned
3. We just hit [milestone] — here's exactly what changed
4. The thing I was most wrong about when I started
5. Unfiltered: what it actually looks like building [thing]
6. I almost gave up on [month]. Here's what happened.
7. Revenue update: [Month] was [good/bad/weird] — breakdown inside
8. What I wish I'd known before starting [thing]
9. We made a big decision this week — here's why
10. Behind the scenes: how we ship content in [X hours/week]

### Claude Prompt for Building in Public

```
Write a [LinkedIn post / TikTok script / Twitter thread] in a building-in-public style.

Rules:
- Share a real number, milestone, or failure (make it specific)
- Be honest about what's hard, not just what's working
- The "lesson" should be unexpected, not obvious
- Avoid inspirational-poster conclusions
- End with an open question to spark comments
- Pillar: Building in Public
- Tone: Honest, grounded, conversational
```

### Hook Formulas

- "I'm going to share something I've never posted publicly before:"
- "[Timeframe] ago I [state]. Today: [different state]. What changed:"
- "Honest update: [Month] was the hardest month of the year. Here's why."

## Pillar 3 — Industry Insights (20%)

**Goal:** Be the person who synthesizes trends and data so others don't have to.

**What performs:** Trend predictions with reasoning, data that reframes a common belief, analysis of a big industry event.

### 10 Evergreen Topic Ideas

1. The trend that will define [industry] in the next 12 months
2. What [major platform update] means for marketers (the real impact)
3. [Stat]: what this number tells us about where [market] is heading
4. Why AI [won't/will] replace [role] in the next 3 years
5. The quiet shift happening in B2B buying behavior
6. What [major brand] gets right that most companies miss
7. Why [popular strategy] is about to stop working
8. The metric every marketer should be tracking but isn't
9. What 500 hours of studying top creators taught me
10. The uncomfortable truth about [topic] that no one says out loud

### Claude Prompt for Industry Insights

```
Write a [LinkedIn post / TikTok script / Twitter thread] sharing an industry insight or trend analysis.

Rules:
- Lead with a data point, study, or observation — cite it even if vague ("according to a recent report...")
- Explain WHY this trend is happening (deeper than the surface)
- Give the implication: "What this means for you:"
- 1-2 contrarian or unexpected takes
- Pillar: Industry Insights
- Tone: Analyst meets strategist — smart, credible, not boring
```

### Hook Formulas

- "[X%] of [professionals] are doing [thing] wrong. The data is clear."
- "Something big is shifting in [industry]. Most people haven't noticed yet."
- "I've been watching [trend] for 6 months. Here's what I see coming:"

## Pillar 4 — Hot Takes (20%)

**Goal:** Polarize and entertain. Get shares from people who agree, comments from people who disagree.

**What performs:** Contrarian opinions on established best practices, debunking popular advice, defending unpopular positions.

### 10 Evergreen Topic Ideas

1. [Popular advice] is actually terrible advice for most founders
2. The growth hack era is dead. Here's what actually works now.
3. Most "personal brand" content is just noise. Here's the difference.
4. Stop optimizing for followers. Optimize for [this] instead.
5. Cold email is not dead. You're just doing it wrong.
6. Why I deleted [metric] from my dashboard (and you should too)
7. The worst career advice I ever received (from a "successful" person)
8. Most content calendars are a waste of time. Fight me.
9. The myth of "just provide value" and why it's holding you back
10. [Conventional wisdom] worked 5 years ago. It doesn't anymore.

### Claude Prompt for Hot Takes

```
Write a [LinkedIn post / TikTok script / Twitter thread] with a bold, contrarian take.

Rules:
- State the hot take clearly in the first sentence — no hedging
- Back it up with 2-3 specific reasons or examples
- Acknowledge the counterargument, then dismiss it
- Don't walk it back at the end — commit to the take
- Invite debate in the final line
- Pillar: Hot Takes
- Tone: Confident, direct, a little provocative — never mean
```

### Hook Formulas

- "Unpopular opinion: [bold statement about a widely accepted practice]"
- "[Commonly praised thing] is overrated. I'll explain."
- "I'm going to get pushback for this: [contrarian take]"

## Pillar 5 — Personal (10%)

**Goal:** Be a human, not a brand. Build the emotional connection that makes everything else land.

**What performs:** Values stories, formative moments, behind-the-scenes glimpses, honest reflections on career or life.

### 10 Evergreen Topic Ideas

1. The moment I knew I had to leave [job/industry]
2. What my dad/mom taught me about [value] that shapes how I run my business
3. The decision that scared me most — and why I made it anyway
4. I don't talk about this much, but [personal thing] affects how I work
5. Year in review: the wins, the losses, the things I'm still processing
6. What I do when I feel like giving up (it's not motivational)
7. My actual morning routine (not the aspirational version)
8. The skill that has nothing to do with business and makes me better at it
9. The person who changed how I think about [thing]
10. What I'm proud of that nobody claps for

### Claude Prompt for Personal

```
Write a [LinkedIn post / TikTok script / Twitter thread] that's personal and human.

Rules:
- Share a real story with a specific scene or moment — not a general reflection
- Include at least one detail that makes it feel lived (a name, a place, a date)
- The "lesson" should emerge from the story, not be stated like a moral
- Don't make it about productivity or business performance
- End with something that invites the reader to share their own experience
- Pillar: Personal
- Tone: Warm, honest, grounded — never inspirational-poster
```

### Hook Formulas

- "The worst day of my professional life happened on a [day] in [year]."
- "I don't talk about this often, but [personal revelation]."
- "Something my [person] said to me [timeframe] ago changed how I work:"

## Hook Formulas by Platform

### LinkedIn (Professional)
- Lead with the insight, not the setup
- Numbers and specifics in line 1 perform better than questions
- Avoid "I'm excited to share..." — start with the value

### Twitter/X (Hook tweet must work standalone)
- 1 idea per tweet — don't bury the lead
- The first tweet of a thread needs to work as a standalone post
- Use ":" at the end of tweet 1 to signal a thread

### TikTok / Reels (Spoken word)
- Start mid-thought: "Here's the thing about X..."
- First 2 words determine whether they keep watching — make them count
- Pattern interrupt: state something unexpected in the first 3 seconds
